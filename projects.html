IMPACT-A-THON

A competition organized by Carnegie Mellon the college of engineering, aiming to implement positive impact in the society to foster acceptance of disabled children. The prompt of the 2016 competition is to create an accessible playground for children with disabilities. We worked in a group of 5, with groupmates from disciplines all across Statistics, Electrical Engineering, and Mechanical Engineering. Our solution is to create tracks around playground that would allow children with difficulty to walk to be on a cart and enjoy the sensations that the playground facilities would bring.

TARTAN DATA SCIENCE CUP

A data science competition organized by the Statistics department, sponsered by 85.31. The prompt of the competition is for participating teams to predict probability of whether specific 300 households will buy eggs in the following week, given their transaction history. In the given 8 hours, me and my teammate Cissy Shi, who's from computational finance background, derive a prediction model using regression.

DATABASE BUILDING

Computing and Statistics come hand in hand, and a place to store data therefore seemed most important. Noticing the gap in my Statistics study, I choose to go to harvard university for summer school, learning how to build relational database using SQL and phpMyAdmin. The art involved is of course, more than just clicking buttons. I learned how to write queries, analyzing transcript, normalizing entries. By the end, I completed a project with a local wholesalers in building databases the suited their needs.
